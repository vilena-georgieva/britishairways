Welcome to New Orleans!

New Orleans is famous for lots of things for the French Quarter, for Bourbon Street, for Creole cuisine and for jazz, but there’s one event the city is most famous for Mardi Gras!

Mardi Gras is a season of parties, music, fun, parades and all out craziness. Mardi Gras is a time when the entire city comes together for one huge blowout. From the flamboyant costumes to the street parties, New Orleans Mardi Gras has it all! It really is an event that has to be experienced to be understood.

Beyond Mardi Gras, the city is a blossoming cultural hub, bursting with the sights, sounds and tastes to keep every visitor happy.

    Troubadour

    Serving up good food, great music and a colourful personality, relax in the rooftop bar with stunning city views. Dine on superb local comfort cuisine in the hotel restaurant and embrace southern charm. Explore the neighbouring theatre scene and famous areas of the city, soaking up its heavy musical influence and eclectic personality.

    Troubadour's central location offers impressive views of the famous Theatre district, Garden District and French Quarter from its panoramic rooftop bar. Immerse yourself in the friendly and playful environment of New Orleans, with its music, food and atmosphere, using this modern hotel as a central base to explore the rich culture of this vibrant city.

    Try some of the finest local cuisine in the Phillip Lopez-inspired restaurants, such as crab-stuffed devilled eggs, Steak au Poivre or lobster dogs, all complemented with a full selection of wines. Relax with a drink in the horseshoe shaped bar or head to the rooftop and try street food bites, while soaking up the city views of the Big Easy.

    Relax on pillow top beds and Egyptian cotton sheets. Take in the city views from your window, use the free WiFi to find things to do and enjoy the local artwork adorning the walls. Take advantage of the 24-hour room service and enjoy great food and drinks without leaving your room. Bring the sounds of New Orleans into your room via Bluetooth speaker. Or retreat to your bathroom to make use of the exclusive Jonathan Adler amenities.

    Close to live shows by local musicians, plentiful southern cuisine and tourist sights, start your New Orleans holiday at the Troubadour.
