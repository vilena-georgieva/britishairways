Welcome to New Orleans!

New Orleans is famous for lots of things for the French Quarter, for Bourbon Street, for Creole cuisine and for jazz, but there’s one event the city is most famous for Mardi Gras!

Mardi Gras is a season of parties, music, fun, parades and all out craziness. Mardi Gras is a time when the entire city comes together for one huge blowout. From the flamboyant costumes to the street parties, New Orleans Mardi Gras has it all! It really is an event that has to be experienced to be understood.

Beyond Mardi Gras, the city is a blossoming cultural hub, bursting with the sights, sounds and tastes to keep every visitor happy.

    Hampton Inn & Suites Convention Center

    Set in an early 1900’s cotton mill, this iconic hotel is conveniently located in the historic Arts and Warehouse District. It is an ideal base to explore this vibrant area – within walking distance of the French Quarter, allowing easy access to the Big Easy’s restaurants, shops and attractions.
    This great building houses 288 spacious, contemporary rooms featuring complimentary WiFi, Cloud Nine bedding, a comfortable Hampton bed®, microwave and refrigerator. After a good night’s sleep, treat yourself to a complimentary continental breakfast before heading out to see the sights.
    Treat yourself to a refreshing drink in the charming New Orleans style courtyard, while enjoying the Louisiana sunshine. Or cool down in the attractive outdoor pool.
    Popular attractions include Riverwalk Market Place, Louisiana Children's Museum, National World War II Museum, Riverboat Cruises and the Mercedes-Benz Superdome. Have a bite to eat at one of the many premier dining venues in the lively French Quarter or catch a live band on Bourbon Street.
